Embarrassing Interrogation


Shyly, Patrick entered the police station accompanied by his grandmother. Unlike practically every crime series on TV, you couldn't just walk through but had to sign in at a front desk where an officer sat behind (presumably bulletproof) glass, taking down personal details before the visitors could state their business.

After the officer at the reception made a brief phone call, they were soon picked up by a woman, presumably in her late thirties and in plain clothes, who led them through a security door, into the stairwell and into her office. She introduced herself as the lead inspector of the murder commission. She had taken over the investigation after the police were alerted by a neighbor who had found Xaver Yaris's body when he noticed the open door on his way to work.

The inspector listened attentively to Patrick's account while Grandma Erika kept discreetly in the background.

"Patrick... may I address you with your first name?" the inspector began and continued when Patrick nodded briefly. "You realize you behaved quite clumsily?"

Patrick nodded again.

"I'm not blaming you for your panicked reaction, but if you had called us right away instead of conducting your own investigation..." The inspector broke off and threw a reproachful look at Grandma Erika. "Then we wouldn't have been hunting for someone who apparently moved the body after the murder and left a lot of fingerprints. (We will have to send you to the lab later for fingerprint matching.) Moreover, we would now have a memory stick with important information."

"...and possibly another corpse," countered Grandma Erika. "You heard him; the murderer was still in the house."

"...the boy assumes," the inspector said unmoved. "But no matter! At least you reported it now and saved us further false leads."

"And provided valuable information," Grandma Erika said confidently. "After all, we decrypted the data on the stick."

"...and then had it taken from you!"

"But we re-encrypted the data," Patrick proudly added. "Even if the perpetrator knew the old password or might have cracked it, he will definitely have a hard time with the new one."

"From your lips to God's ears!" the inspector sighed, stretching in her chair.

"What was in it?" she asked, now addressing Erika Podwig. Erika tried to list the names and addresses she could remember and describe the numbers associated with them. She also voiced her suspicion that it might be a case of industrial espionage. The inspector listened attentively and occasionally took a few notes.

Finally, she dismissed them and announced that she would contact Patrick again in the next few days to revisit the crime scene with him and to reconstruct the discovery of the body.
